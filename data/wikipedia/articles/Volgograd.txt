Volgograd (Russian: Волгогра́д, IPA: [vəɫɡɐˈɡrat] ( listen)), formerly Tsaritsyn (Russian:  Цари́цын​ ), 1589–1925, and Stalingrad (Russian:  Сталингра́д​ ), 1925–1961, is an important industrial city and the administrative centre of Volgograd Oblast, Russia, on the western bank of the Volga River. The Battle of Stalingrad in World War II was the largest and bloodiest battle in the history of warfare. Population: 1,021,215 (2010 Census); 1,011,417 (2002 Census); 1,022,578 (1989 Census).
