Sridevi was an Indian film actress who worked in 300 films in Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, Kannada and Hindi.Her acting career started in South Indian Cinema at the age of 4, with the Tamil film Kandan Karunai. She made her transition to adult roles by age 13 with Moondru Mudichu. Due to her immense popularity and pan Indian appeal, she is often cited as the first female superstar of Indian Cinema. Regarded as one of the finest actresses of  Indian Cinema, Sridevi's performances in films in a variety of genres have earned her praise and awards . She took a sabbatical from film acting in 1997. During her career, she won six Filmfare Awards: three for Hindi films, two for Tamil films and one for Telugu films. Her comeback was with English Vinglish in 2012.
