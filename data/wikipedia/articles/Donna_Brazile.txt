Donna Lease Brazile (; born December 15, 1959) is an American political strategist, campaign manager, political analyst, and author. She is a member of the Democratic Party, briefly serving as the interim chairperson for the Democratic National Committee in spring 2011, and assumed that role again in July 2016, until February 2017.
She was the first African American woman to direct a major presidential campaign, acting as campaign manager for Al Gore in 2000.
