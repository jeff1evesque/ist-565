Paul Stephen Rudd (born April 6, 1969) is an American actor, comedian, writer and producer who studied theatre at the University of Kansas and the  American Drama Academy, before making his acting debut in 1992 with NBC's drama series Sisters. He is known for his starring roles in the films Clueless (1995), Romeo + Juliet (1996), Wet Hot American Summer (2001), Anchorman: The Legend of Ron Burgundy (2004), The 40-Year-Old Virgin (2005), Knocked Up (2007), Role Models (2008), I Love You, Man (2009), This Is 40 (2012), Anchorman 2: The Legend Continues (2013) and The Fundamentals of Caring (2016).
In the Marvel Cinematic Universe, he played the superhero Ant-Man in Ant-Man (2015) for which he co-wrote the screenplay, Captain America: Civil War (2016), Ant-Man and the Wasp (2018), again for which he co-wrote the screenplay and will return in Avengers 4 (2019).
In addition to his film career, Rudd has appeared in numerous television shows, including the NBC sitcom Friends as Mike Hannigan, along with guest roles on Tim and Eric Awesome Show, Great Job! and Parks and Recreation (as businessman Bobby Newport) and hosting Saturday Night Live. Rudd received a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame on July 1, 2015.
