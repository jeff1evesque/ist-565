Ice Age: Collision Course is a 2016 American 3D computer animated comedy film produced by Blue Sky Studios and distributed by 20th Century Fox. It is the fifth installment in the Ice Age film series and the sequel to Ice Age: Continental Drift (2012). It was directed by Mike Thurmeier and Galen Tan Chu, from a screenplay by Michael J. Wilson, Michael Berg and Yoni Brenner, and stars Ray Romano, John Leguizamo, Denis Leary, Queen Latifah Seann William Scott, Josh Peck, and Jennifer Lopez reprising their roles from the previous films, with Jesse Tyler Ferguson, Adam DeVine, Nick Offerman, Max Greenfield, Stephanie Beatriz, Melissa Rauch, Michael Strahan, Jessie J and Neil deGrasse Tyson voicing new characters and Simon Pegg reprising his role as Buck from Ice Age: Dawn of the Dinosaurs. In the film, while trying to bury his acorn, Scrat discovers a UFO and launches it into space, accidentally launching thousands of deadly meteors to Earth during the process. When Manny and the others meet up with Buck and learn that a very deadly meteor is coming, they must go on a life or death mission to find a way to stop it.
The film premiered at the Sydney Film Festival on June 19, 2016, and was released in the United States on July 22, 2016. Ice Age: Collision Course received generally negative reviews from critics and grossed $408 million worldwide on a $105 million budget.
